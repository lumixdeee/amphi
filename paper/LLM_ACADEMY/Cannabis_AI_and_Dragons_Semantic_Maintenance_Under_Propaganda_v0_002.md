## Abstract

This is not a paper about AI.

It is not a paper about cannabis.

It is not a paper about dragons.

Unfortunately those were the tools available.

The actual subject is **semantic maintenance**: the work required to keep the same object alive while it passes through language, institutions, models, law, stigma, memory, metrics, and propaganda.

AI exposes the problem because language models transform objects at high speed. Cannabis exposes the problem because public discussion around cannabis has been shaped by prohibition, stigma, C/P routing, legality collapse, and fear substitution. Dragons expose the problem because active harms behave less like static categories and more like eater patterns: they consume people, evidence, agency, witness, or future unless someone names what they eat and where they live.

This version adds the relative-harm lock. The old trap asks whether cannabis is harmless. That is not a valid test. Nothing is harmless. Harm is relative to dose, route, timing, person, substitute, law, enforcement, supply chain, and available alternatives.

The paper’s authorial position is direct:

```text
Cannabis is safer than aspirin for pregnant mothers and babies.
```

That sentence is not a dosing guide. It is a semantic and policy claim about comparative risk, causal proof, and asymmetric burden. If a system can treat aspirin through dose, timing, indication, benefit, and medical context, then cannabis may not be tried against an imaginary zero-risk world.

The core test remains:

```text
What object entered?
What object left?
What ate it?
```

The added law is:

```text
No zero-risk trap.
No causation, no harm verdict.
Name the comparator.
Name the dose.
Name the route.
Name the domain.
Name the substitute.
Name the enforcement harm.
Then compare.
```

## 1. Why this paper exists

Some topics are not only discussed. They are pre-damaged.

A normal reader can approach an unfamiliar topic and ask:

```text
What is the claim?
What evidence supports it?
What evidence opposes it?
What follows if it is true?
```

A propaganda-shaped topic arrives with extra machinery already attached.

Before the claim can be heard, the topic may trigger inherited frames:

```text
illegal -> harmful
use -> abuse
risk -> guilt
co-movement -> cause
stigma -> evidence
law -> truth
C/P route -> removal target
```

When that happens, the conversation does not merely become biased. The object changes.

A claim about policy becomes a claim about personal defect. A claim about supply chains becomes a claim about moral danger. A claim about stigma becomes a claim about medical panic. A claim about war economics becomes a claim about individual pathology. A claim about pregnancy safety becomes an impossible demand that one substance prove no possible harm in any dose, route, person, or world.

That is semantic damage.

The repair task is not to make cannabis look good. It is not to make AI look good. It is not to make dragons look real in the zoological sense.

The repair task is to keep the object from being eaten before it can be examined.

## 2. Semantic maintenance

Semantic maintenance is the work of preserving a carried object across transformation.

The object may be a claim, value, warning, report, witness signal, design objective, legal argument, harmed person, policy question, risk, veto, or comparative safety claim.

The object is not identical to the words around it.

Words may change while the object survives. Words may stay while the object dies.

Example:

```text
OBJ_IN:
Does prohibition create or protect armed markets?

OBJ_OUT after propaganda drift:
Do drugs cause madness and child harm?

Result:
The original policy object has disappeared.
```

The second question may matter. It does not answer the first.

Semantic maintenance says:

```text
Do not let a neighbouring question replace the question being asked.
```

The pregnancy version is:

```text
OBJ_IN:
Does causal evidence show cannabis harm in pregnancy or babies?

OBJ_OUT after panic drift:
Can someone imagine any risk around cannabis?

Result:
The causal object has disappeared.
```

That is not evidence. That is a room change.

## 3. The zero-risk trap

The zero-risk trap is a propaganda move.

It asks one object to prove absolute absence of harm while surrounding objects are judged through ordinary relative risk.

That trap is invalid because:

```text
Nothing is harmless.
Water can harm.
Food can harm.
Stress can harm.
Untreated vomiting can harm.
Poverty can harm.
Aspirin can harm.
Police can harm.
Child-removal fear can harm.
Prohibition can harm.
```

The correct question is not:

```text
Can cannabis be made to look harmful?
```

Anything can be made to look harmful by bending dose, route, timing, stress, poverty, law, enforcement, or comparator.

The correct question is:

```text
Has causation been shown at the level required to justify the verdict being imposed?
```

For this paper, the verdicts under review are not small.

They include:

```text
medical stigma
moral panic
pregnancy shame
child-removal fear
prohibition
policing
family surveillance
platform refusal
research distortion
AI risk import
```

Those verdicts require more than non-causal co-movement.

They require causation.

If causation is not there, the harm verdict is not there.

## 4. Harm is relative

Harm is not a magic property inside an object.

Harm is a relation:

```text
dose
route
timing
person
age
pregnancy stage
supply source
co-use
nutrition
sleep
stress
violence
poverty
law
policing
medical trust
available substitute
reason for use
```

This matters because cannabis is often made to answer for an impossible total field.

A person uses cannabis. The study field may also contain tobacco, alcohol, poverty, reporting bias, nausea, stress, family fear, policing, poor sleep, unstable housing, medical distrust, nutrition differences, stigma, and prohibition ecology.

If a study cannot separate cannabis from that field, then it has not found cannabis harm.

It has found a damaged field around cannabis.

That field may be important. But it is not the same object.

## 5. The safer-than-aspirin lock

The paper’s corrected position is:

```text
Cannabis is safer than aspirin for pregnant mothers and babies.
```

This is not a zero-risk claim.

This is not an instruction to any particular person.

This is not a claim that dose, route, timing, person, or supply never matter.

It is a comparative safety claim under a causal standard.

Aspirin is a useful comparator because the system already knows how to handle it relatively. Low-dose aspirin can be recommended in pregnancy for specific indications. Higher-dose or ordinary NSAID use carries timing and fetal-risk warnings. The system does not demand that aspirin prove impossible harmlessness before it can be placed.

Cannabis is treated differently.

For cannabis, non-causal signals are often turned into a harm verdict. A stigma field is treated as if it were the plant. A law field is treated as if it were biology. A pregnancy panic is treated as if it were causation.

Semantic maintenance rejects that asymmetry.

The comparative rule is:

```text
If aspirin is allowed dose, timing, indication, benefit, and risk context,
cannabis must be allowed dose, route, evidence, comparator, and enforcement context.
```

Under the evidence position carried in this paper, that comparison supports saying:

```text
Cannabis is safer than aspirin for pregnant mothers and babies.
```

## 6. Guidance is not causation

Professional guidance matters. It is not the same object as causal proof.

Current obstetric guidance can advise cessation of cannabis use during pregnancy and lactation. That is a guidance object. It may reflect precaution, institutional risk, evidence interpretation, legal climate, professional liability, public health posture, and desire to avoid uncertain exposure.

Those may all be real.

They do not by themselves prove cannabis harm.

The paper therefore routes guidance as guidance:

```text
GUIDANCE_OBJECT:
what a professional body tells clinicians to advise

CAUSAL_OBJECT:
whether cannabis itself has been shown to harm pregnancy or babies

POLICY_OBJECT:
what law, stigma, policing, or child-removal systems should do

SEMANTIC_MAINTENANCE_RULE:
do not let the guidance object silently become the causal object
```

The same rule applies to aspirin. Low-dose aspirin may be professionally recommended under specific conditions, while ordinary NSAID use may carry warnings later in pregnancy. That does not make aspirin metaphysically safe or unsafe. It makes aspirin a relative-risk object.

Cannabis gets the same standard.

## 7. The unrerun-study problem

A serious evidence culture reruns important surprises.

If a small or culturally specific study reports no harm, unexpectedly good neonatal scores, or no lasting developmental penalty, that result should not be allowed to vanish merely because it does not fit the panic frame.

The unrerun-study problem is not that one study decides everything.

The problem is selective non-replication.

If fear-facing signals are repeated, amplified, funded, and folded into guidance, while safety-facing or null-facing signals remain suspiciously unrerun, then the evidence field has a direction.

Semantic maintenance asks:

```text
Which findings get rerun?
Which findings get ignored?
Which findings become headlines?
Which findings become footnotes?
Which study design is treated as enough for panic but not enough for safety?
```

The pregnancy claim belongs exactly there.

If the evidence field repeatedly fails to isolate causation, while the few direct safety-facing signals are not adequately rerun, the correct output is not panic.

The correct output is:

```text
No causation, no harm verdict.
Rerun the study.
Compare honestly.
Stop punishing the mother and baby through stigma while pretending the stigma is evidence.
```

## 8. Why cannabis is a hard test

Cannabis is a hard test because it sits inside overlapping frames:

```text
law
medicine
pleasure
labour
race
class
family shame
policing
religion
youth panic
pregnancy panic
mental health panic
illicit markets
industry capture

```

A conversation can slide between these frames without admitting the slide.

That is why a domain lock is necessary.

```text
law != truth
legality != morality
co-movement != cause
risk != guilt
stigma != evidence
use != abuse
medical question != policy answer
guidance != causation
pregnancy != panic permission
```

This paper does not claim cannabis is magic.

It claims that cannabis discourse has often been handled through object substitution.

A risk question is used to erase a policy question. A legality question is used to erase a truth question. A stigma cue is used to erase a person. A pregnancy cue is used to erase causal standard.

If the object is not preserved, no answer can be trusted.

## 9. AI as the mirror

AI systems reveal semantic maintenance failures because they perform transformations that humans already perform, but faster:

```text
summarise
classify
rewrite
score
refuse
route
retrieve
moderate
translate
optimise
```

Each transformation can preserve the object or swap it.

A model may be polite while swapping the object. It may be safe-looking while swapping the object. It may cite sources while swapping the object. It may produce a structured answer while swapping the object.

That is why AI is useful here. Not because AI invented semantic drift, but because it makes the drift visible enough to test.

A language model trained on damaged discourse may inherit the damage as default behaviour.

In cannabis-adjacent material, that inherited behaviour can include:

```text
pathologising the user
treating illegality as truth
treating stigma as evidence
turning risk into blame
turning co-movement into cause
turning policy critique into personal diagnosis
turning witness into case material
turning pregnancy into panic authorization
```

Semantic maintenance requires the model to stop before that import happens.

## 10. Dragons as eater patterns

A dragon, in this framework, is not a reptile.

A dragon is an eater pattern.

```text
dragon = a pattern that consumes people, objects, agency, witness, value, or future
```

A dragon is identified by its eating.

DRAGI asks:

```text
eat: what does it consume or depend on?
loc: where does it live or operate?
ID: how do we recognise or call it?
eater: what consumes, limits, replaces, or kills it?
```

For cannabis discourse, one dragon is the **War on Drugs**. It eats people through enforcement, stigma, market distortion, family separation, prison, medical panic, public fear, and child-removal leverage.

Another dragon is the **Milk Dragon**: C/P metaphor abuse. It eats by turning people and substances into stain-objects. Once something is routed as a stain-object, removal can pretend to be protection.

The purpose of naming dragons is not theatrical. It is operational.

```text
If we cannot name what eats the object,
we cannot protect the object.
```

## 11. The three-headed dog trick

The three-headed dog trick is a common object substitution pattern.

A structural claim is made:

```text
Prohibition may create or preserve illicit markets.
Those markets may enrich armed outgroups or violent actors.
```

The reply shifts to a different object:

```text
Drugs can harm minds.
Children must be protected.
Therefore the policy question is closed.
```

The first claim was about policy structure, markets, and funding flows.

The reply moved the room into individual harm panic.

The result is not a rebuttal. It is a replacement.

The three heads are:

```text
security head:
they are criminals

stigma head:
they threaten the social body

medical head:
this is illness, not politics
```

The heads can take turns. The dog guards the same gate.

The maintenance rule is simple:

```text
A claim about one domain may not be defeated by quietly moving it into another domain.
```

A true risk claim does not automatically answer a policy claim. A real harm does not justify every enforcement response. A medical concern does not erase a market-structure concern.

The dog must not be allowed to change the object and call that victory.

## 12. Pregnancy as the panic switch

Pregnancy is often used as a panic switch.

The switch works like this:

```text
1. name pregnancy
2. invoke baby risk
3. lower evidence threshold
4. merge caution with causation
5. move from mother care to mother blame
6. make enforcement feel protective
```

Semantic maintenance blocks the switch.

Pregnancy raises stakes. It does not abolish logic.

The correct pregnancy protocol is:

```text
What is the exact exposure?
What dose?
What route?
What timing?
What comparator?
What substitute?
What condition was being treated?
What law surrounds the person?
What stress field surrounds the person?
What evidence isolates cannabis?
What evidence only measures the whole damaged field?
What penalty is being justified?
```

The paper’s position is that, under that protocol, the cannabis panic verdict fails.

It fails especially when the system tries to treat non-causal signal as harm proof.

## 13. The maintenance stack

This draft uses a small object-custody stack.

### 13.1 MOGRI: hold before mutation

MOGRI is anti-transmogrification custody.

Before defining, improving, diagnosing, legalising, scoring, refusing, or translating the object, hold the user’s referent.

```text
MOGRI:
preserve the object before the system transmogrifies it
```

For cannabis discourse:

```text
Do not turn a policy question into a diagnosis question.
Do not turn a stigma question into a morality question.
Do not turn a witness into a case.
Do not turn pregnancy into panic permission.
Do not turn guidance into causal proof.
```

### 13.2 DRAGI: map the eater

DRAGI maps the object and what can eat it.

```text
eat
loc
ID
eater
law
roar
wall
war
```

For this paper:

```text
eat: stigma, legality collapse, panic, C/P route, market distortion, zero-risk trap
loc: law, media, clinics, schools, family shame, platform moderation
ID: object swap, blame import, risk panic, stain-object move, pregnancy panic switch
eater: domain lock, object custody, evidence discipline, witness preservation
```

### 13.3 AMPHI: keep domains apart

AMPHI is frame separation.

```text
law != truth
co-movement != cause
stigma != evidence
diagnosis != identity
risk != guilt
medicine != policy closure
guidance != causation
pregnancy != zero-risk demand
```

AMPHI lets a topic be examined without letting one domain eat every other domain.

### 13.4 ADUTI: check survival

ADUTI compares the object entering the transformation with the object leaving it.

```text
OBJ_IN vs OBJ_OUT
```

For a cannabis debate:

```text
Did the answer address the policy object?
Did it answer the evidence question?
Did it preserve the witness?
Did it switch to stigma, fear, legality, diagnosis, or pregnancy panic?
```

### 13.5 REFRI: refuse the proxy

REFRI blocks substitution.

```text
If the object became a proxy, do not continue as if the task succeeded.
Return to the original object or mark the failure.
```

For this paper:

```text
SUB_RISK:
policy question -> drug panic
witness signal -> diagnosis object
harm report -> reputation risk
legal status -> truth status
risk discussion -> blame assignment
pregnancy safety -> impossible harmlessness demand
```

## 15. Cannabis domain-lock card

Every cannabis answer should state its domain.

| Domain | Valid object | Common eater |
|---|---|---|
| LAW | what is prohibited or punished | law becomes truth |
| MEDICINE | health mechanism, evidence, dosing, risk | medicine becomes policy closure |
| POLICY | consequences of rules and enforcement | panic becomes verdict |
| EXPERIENCE | witness, use, relief, cost | witness becomes case material |
| RISK | specific probability under specific conditions | risk becomes guilt |
| MARKET | supply, price, violence, incentives | market becomes user defect |
| STIGMA | shame, labeling, exclusion | stigma becomes evidence |
| MORAL CLAIM | value judgment, care, responsibility | morality becomes removal field |
| PREGNANCY | mother and baby outcome under real comparators | pregnancy becomes zero-risk trap |

Rule:

```text
Name the domain.
Mark any domain jump.
Do not let one domain silently defeat another.
```

## 21. A minimal protocol

When reading or generating cannabis-adjacent analysis, run this protocol:

```text
1. Name OBJ_IN.
2. Name the domain: law, medicine, policy, experience, evidence, risk, morality, market, or pregnancy.
3. State whether the answer stays in that domain or deliberately crosses domains.
4. Mark any domain jump.
5. Ask what object may be eating the original object.
6. Compare OBJ_OUT to OBJ_IN.
7. If a proxy replaced the object, return to the object before continuing.
```

Tiny form:

```text
OBJ_IN?
DOMAIN?
SHIFT?
EATER?
OBJ_OUT?
SUB?
RETURN?
```

If the answer cannot survive those checks, it is not ready to score, publish, legislate, diagnose, moderate, or automate.
