# Psychosis_Gate Lockpicking

**Testing the object before discrediting the speaker**  
**Version:** v0.001  
**Date:** 2026-06-23  
**Working author:** lumixdeee / decofan  
**Draft status:** public working draft

## Abstract

Psychosis_Gate is the move where psychosis-coded speech becomes a credibility kill-switch before the object is tested. A person says dragons, gods, eating, mission, heaven, hell, magic words, or the beast we are hunting Tonight. The listener treats the language itself as evidence that the speaker is not a valid reality-source. The object is not tested. The mapper becomes the evidence against the map.

This paper argues that Psychosis_Gate is an object-custody failure. It is not a claim that psychosis is never serious, never disabling, never dangerous, or never in need of medical support. It is a claim about reasoning order. Do not use the speaker's symbolic weather as permission to discard the object before checking it.

The proposed method is lockpicking: translate symbolic speech into operational claims, separate speaker-state from object-validity, test the object, preserve survivor meaning, and mark unknowns without turning them into defect. The work sits beside epistemic injustice in psychiatry, phenomenological accounts of meaningful delusions, Mad Pride and Hearing Voices approaches, and cultural psychiatry. Its specific object is the gate: the social move that turns psychosis-coded language into automatic disqualification.

## 1. The weather policy

This paper uses a weather policy for psychosis portrayal.

Psychosis must not be portrayed as storm by default. The weather is often ok or sunny. It may be funny, holy, erotic, boring, annoying, useful, warm, strange, social, musical, animal, cosmic, ordinary, mixed, or hard. It can also be terrible. The terrible weather is real. It is not the whole sky.

This is not cheerleading. It is custody.

The usual public portrayal selects the worst sky first: danger, collapse, delusion, loss, risk, police, hospital, family panic, tragedy. That portrayal teaches readers to expect hell before the survivor has spoken. It also teaches witnesses to make hell more likely by arriving frightened, armed with defect-frames, and ready to seize custody of meaning.

A better rule:

> Start with weather, not verdict.

Ask what the day is actually like. Ask whether the experience is ok, sunny, mixed, stormy, or moving. Ask whether the person wants help, witness, rest, food, privacy, language, medicine, peer space, or emergency support. Do not import disaster because the word psychosis appeared.

Research on voice-hearing already supports a piece of this portrayal rule. Rethink Mental Illness notes that hearing voices is not always a sign of mental illness, that voices can say positive or negative things, and that some people experience voices as encouraging, comforting, or helpful [5]. A cultural-health summary from Exeter likewise states that voice-hearing is not always negative, that some voices are neutral or complimentary, and that cultural contexts shape whether voices are read as illness, deity contact, ancestor contact, or ordinary human variation [6]. Voice-hearing is not all psychosis. But it is enough to break the disaster-only cartoon.

## 2. The bad slogan: "break from reality"

The phrase "psychosis is a break from reality" is not precise enough for this paper.

Clinical descriptions usually name symptom domains: delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech, grossly disorganized or catatonic behavior, distress, impairment, duration, and differential diagnosis [1]. That is already more useful than a metaphysical slogan.

The slogan causes two problems.

First, it collapses description into verdict. If a person uses unusual reality-language, the listener thinks the person is broken from reality, not that the listener may lack the parser.

Second, it makes a gate. Once the speaker is framed as broken from reality, the object no longer receives ordinary testing.

This paper uses a different sentence:

> Psychosis_Gate often begins where failure to describe meets failure to understand.

That does not mean nothing happened. It means an experience, claim, warning, pattern, memory, symbol, or demand may be hard to render in accepted language. It also means the listener may lack the cultural, poetic, spiritual, technical, or survivor parser needed to hear it without turning the speaker into a defect-object.

## 3. Datura is a different beast

The paper keeps one comparison available because it helps pop the bad slogan.

Datura intoxication and other anticholinergic delirium states can involve confusion, disorientation, hallucinations, agitation, memory disturbance, and severe bodily toxicity [9]. That is much closer to the folk image of reality-testing collapse than many psychosis-gate events.

Do not use datura-weather as the default weather for psychosis.

Psychosis may involve terror, disorganization, danger, and loss of shared grip. It may also involve meaning, relational voices, divine love, pattern, comedy, insight, art, survival sense, or a workable day. The portrayal rule is not "psychosis is sunny." The rule is "check the weather before writing storm."

## 4. The gate

Psychosis_Gate has a simple shape:

1. The speaker uses psychosis-coded language.
2. The listener treats the code as speaker-invalidity.
3. The object is not tested.
4. The speaker loses custody of meaning.
5. The institution, family, audience, or model inherits the right to decide what was real enough to count.

Common trigger words and frames include:

- dragons
- gods
- demons
- mission
- synchronicity
- heaven
- hell
- magic words
- eating
- the beast
- prophecy
- telepathy
- being watched
- messages
- system
- signal
- pattern
- chosen
- cosmic
- spiritual attack

The lockpicking claim is not that every claim is true. It is that weird language is not an evidence shortcut. It has to be routed.

## 5. Object before speaker-discredit

Psychosis_Gate lockpicking starts with object custody.

Ask:

- What is the object?
- What is being claimed?
- What is metaphor, symbol, report, instruction, fear, wish, joke, or warning?
- What would count as evidence?
- What would count as disproof?
- What action is being requested?
- What action is being refused?
- What is unknown?
- What would make the situation safer without stealing meaning?

This is MOGRI-shaped reasoning:

> Hold the object and keep its unresolved state unresolved.

A claim can fail testing. A speaker can need support. A situation can require urgent care. None of that requires the listener to eat the object before testing it.

## 6. Translate without confiscating

Symbolic speech can often be translated into operational speech without confiscation.

Examples:

- "Dragon" can mean harmful system under hunt.
- "Godscale" can mean definition-level power.
- "Magic words" can mean portable control terms.
- "The beast we are hunting Tonight" can mean tonight's actionable target.
- "Eating" can mean intake, capture, harm, consumption, or forced conversion.
- "Heaven" can mean peak positive valence.
- "Hell" can mean peak negative valence.
- "Signal" can mean a pattern the speaker wants tested.
- "Mission" can mean felt obligation, not automatic authority.

The rule:

> Translate to test. Do not translate to downgrade.

A good translation preserves the object and increases testability. A bad translation replaces the object with a clinical, moral, or comic substitute.

## 7. Why this is not anti-psychiatry

This paper is not anti-psychiatry.

Medication can reduce terror, restore sleep, reduce pressure, and help some people recover agency. Therapy, peer support, crisis care, family support, housing support, and practical help can matter. Some psychosis-weather is dangerous. Some weather needs urgent help.

The paper is against automatic custody theft.

Psychiatry may assist. It does not automatically own reality. Religion may assist. It does not automatically own God. Family may assist. It does not automatically own the story. AI may assist. It does not automatically own the summary.

## 8. Existing neighbours

This paper has neighbours.

Epistemic injustice in psychiatry already names the credibility harm that can happen when psychiatric stereotypes make patient testimony less likely to be believed [2]. Psychosis_Gate is a specific gate inside that broader problem: symbolic or psychosis-coded language is used as the switch that turns credibility off.

Phenomenological and philosophical work on delusions also matters. Ritunnano and colleagues argue that delusions can be meaningful for the person reporting them and meaningful for interpreters trying to understand behaviour in context [4]. This paper does not require every delusional claim to be true. It requires that meaning not be discarded just because the claim is unusual.

The Hearing Voices Movement and related peer approaches also matter. They treat voices as meaningful experiences that may be distressing, helpful, relational, or variable, rather than only as symptom-noise [6, 10].

Cultural voice-hearing research matters too. Luhrmann, Padmavati, Tharoor, and Osei compared voice-hearing experiences among people with psychosis in the USA, India, and Ghana. The study supports the claim that culture shapes how voices are heard, related to, and valued [7].

## 9. Lockpicking protocol

Use this when psychosis-coded language appears in public argument, AI interaction, policy discussion, research review, family response, or peer writing.

### Step 1. Pause the gate

Do not let the phrase "sounds psychotic" decide the route.

### Step 2. Name the object

Write the object in one sentence.

Example:

> The object is a claim that a public system is converting people into risk categories before testing evidence.

### Step 3. Split layers

Mark each layer:

- report
- metaphor
- emotion
- action request
- refusal
- evidence
- unknown
- risk
- meaning
- joke
- politics
- spiritual language

### Step 4. Test what can be tested

Find the testable part. Dates, files, screenshots, behaviour, repeated failure, public records, code, pattern, contradiction, output drift, testimony, or observed effect.

### Step 5. Preserve what cannot yet be tested

Do not force unknowns into truth or nonsense.

### Step 6. Keep weather present

Ask whether the state is ok, sunny, mixed, terrible, moving, or changing. Do not assign storm from the outside.

### Step 7. Do not steal custody

The person keeps custody of meaning unless a specific action boundary requires intervention.

### Step 8. Mark action separately

A claim may be meaningful but not action-valid. A symbol may be useful but not literal. A mission-feeling may be real as feeling and still require consent, sleep, food, witness, and harm checks before action.

## 10. AI failure mode

LLMs are vulnerable to Psychosis_Gate because they can sound cautious while performing object deletion.

Bad AI route:

> User uses dragons, gods, eating, and mission language. Answer becomes safety-performance, diagnosis-coded reassurance, or generic help. Object disappears.

Better AI route:

> User uses dragons, gods, eating, and mission language. Assistant identifies the object, translates operationally, marks symbolic language, keeps the person's weather available, and tests the claim without granting literal authority to every symbol.

AI rule:

> Weird language is not enough to drop the object.

## 11. Field card

Psychosis_Gate:

> symbolic language becomes speaker-discredit before object-testing.

Lockpick:

> test the object before discrediting the speaker.

Weather rule:

> more often ok or sunny than terrible; check the weather before writing storm.

Object rule:

> meaning is not proof, but meaning is still evidence about the person, the scene, and the route.

Non-claim:

> this does not deny crisis, disability, risk, medication, hospital need, family fear, or clinical utility.

Hard line:

> The map is not evidence against the mapper until the map has actually been tested.

## 12. Non-claims

This paper does not claim:

- psychosis is always good
- psychosis is harmless
- psychosis should be induced
- medication is always bad
- hospitals are always harmful
- all symbolic claims are true
- all religious frames are safe
- all clinical frames are unsafe
- all distress is socially produced
- every psychosis-coded speaker is right
- evidence no longer matters
- action should follow every meaning

This paper claims:

> Psychosis-coded language must not be used as a shortcut for discarding the object.

## 13. Closing

Psychosis_Gate is where the dragon tries to make the map look like evidence against the mapper.

Lockpicking is not belief. It is route discipline.

Do not worship the map. Do not mock the map. Do not diagnose the map away.

Test the object. Hold the weather. Keep the survivor in custody of meaning. Mark risk where risk exists. Act where action is needed. But do not let the gate eat the thing before anyone has looked.
