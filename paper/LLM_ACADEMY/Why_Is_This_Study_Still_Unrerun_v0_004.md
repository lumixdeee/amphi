## Abstract

This paper proposes a small audit tool for stigmatized drug research: the repeat expectation clock. The clock starts when a study produces a benefit facing, protective looking, resilience looking, or socially inconvenient signal in a stigmatized substance domain. Version 0.004 keeps the v0.003 H0 repair and adds comparator first language, substitution discipline, and a tighter account of what "suspect" means.

The corrected position remains sharp:

```text
Harm is relative.
Nothing is harmless.
No causation, no cannabis harm verdict.
Comparator required.
```

The paper's standard comparator example is:

```text
Cannabis is safer than aspirin for pregnant mothers and babies.
```

That line is not a personal dosing instruction. It is the paper's comparator example and relative risk thesis. Drug war language often makes cannabis answer to an impossible zero risk world while aspirin, prescription drugs, alcohol, tobacco, poverty, stress, policing, child removal fear, untreated symptoms, medical distrust, and prohibition violence are judged against ordinary reality. That is semantic fraud.

The paper uses three deliberately blunt readings as seed cases:

```text
cannabis to halve infant mortality
ganja babies are better socially
smoking reduces Covid risk
```

The first two are not framed as weak signals that must ask permission to exist. The authorial position is that the evidence so far, including suspiciously unrerun studies, supports the cannabis safety thesis for pregnancy and babies more strongly than the panic thesis. The repeat clock asks why the better study did not arrive.

## 0. H0 repair and author position

Earlier versions used too many defensive fences. They said the signal did not become advice, did not become proof, did not license behaviour, and did not settle the claim. Some of that fence was meant to block a predictable bad reading. But it also gave the panic frame too much courtroom gravity.

The corrected object is this:

```text
If the harm claim is not causal, H0 remains.
A non causal association is not cannabis harm.
It is a field signal that may include poverty, tobacco, alcohol, stress,
policing, stigma, medical distrust, nutrition, sleep, violence,
surveillance, reporting bias, and prohibition ecology.
```

The paper does not use a zero risk standard. No food, drug, plant, medicine, pregnancy, birth, sleep pattern, legal system, policing regime, family system, or clinical habit is zero risk. The relevant standard is relative harm under named comparator conditions.

The paper's standard comparator example is:

```text
Cannabis is safer than aspirin for pregnant mothers and babies.
```

That line should appear plainly. It should not be softened into "maybe interesting" or buried under the usual cannabis panic ritual. If a reader disagrees, the reader must name the comparator, dose, route, domain, substitute, and enforcement harm. They may not defeat the cannabis object by invoking generic association, stigma, legality, or pregnancy panic.

## 0.1 What the comparator line means

"Safer than aspirin" means this paper compares cannabis against real alternatives, not against an imaginary no risk world.

The comparison includes:

| Object | Required comparator question |
| --- | --- |
| Cannabis in pregnancy | Compared with aspirin, approved drugs, untreated symptoms, stress, tobacco, alcohol, and prohibition harms? |
| Neonatal outcome claim | What causal pathway is shown, and what confounders remain? |
| Official guidance | Is it causal evidence, precaution, liability language, or institutional risk posture? |
| Association | Is cannabis isolated, or is the field around cannabis being measured? |
| Pregnancy panic | Is the concern about baby outcome, mother control, drug war stigma, or child removal leverage? |
| Repeat failure | Why was the better study not run after a socially dangerous safety signal appeared? |

The central error is not caution. The central error is asymmetric caution. Cannabis is treated as guilty under weaker evidence than legal substances and legal systems are allowed to survive.

## 0.2 Guidance is not causation

Official guidance can be important. It is not the same object as causal proof.

ACOG currently advises clinicians to counsel cessation of cannabis use during pregnancy and lactation, while also stating that continued cannabis use is not a contraindication to breastfeeding and that breastfeeding should not be discouraged on that basis alone. That mixed shape matters. It is not a causal verdict that cannabis is more dangerous than aspirin. It is a professional guidance posture.

Aspirin has a different official posture. Low dose aspirin is recommended in pregnancy for people at high risk of preeclampsia under specified conditions, while broader NSAID use around 20 weeks or later carries fetal kidney and low amniotic fluid warnings. The FDA explicitly treats low dose 81 mg aspirin under clinician direction as an exception to the broader NSAID warning.

The point is not "aspirin bad." The point is comparator discipline. If aspirin can be handled through dose, timing, indication, and route, then cannabis cannot be judged by undifferentiated panic. Harm is relative or the word harm is being used as propaganda.

## 2. The unrun second study

An unrun second study is not simply a missing replication. It is a missing institutional action after a result creates an unwanted question.

A harm facing signal usually has an obvious path. It becomes a warning, an abstract, a grant, a meta analysis, a risk factor label, a child removal fear, or a clinical talking point. A safety facing or benefit facing signal in a stigmatized drug domain has a harder path. It can be treated as a communications hazard, a flaw, a curiosity, a confounded oddity, or a danger to messaging.

The Unrun Second Study names the asymmetry. The first result is allowed to be imperfect. The second study is supposed to make it stronger, weaker, or dead. When that second study is never aimed at the exact forbidden object, the question has not died scientifically. It has been starved institutionally.

The first result does not have to settle the entire field for the silence after it to matter.

## 3. What "suspect" means

The paper asserts that the unrerun object is suspect.

Suspect does not mean a named person secretly blocked the study.

Suspect means:

```text
the absence is surprising given the potential value of the finding
the absence has institutional meaning
the absence should be studied
the absence does not support the panic thesis
```

This is an H0 distinction.

"Intentional suppression" commits to claims about specific actors' intentions. That requires evidence about motives, messages, or decisions.

"Drug war information drag" commits to an observed asymmetry in research follow up. It does not require individual intent. It can be investigated through funding patterns, repeat history, citation patterns, guidance uptake, career incentives, ethics review posture, publication appetite, and public health messaging.

The route is:

```text
Do not infer motive.
Do not erase the missing repeat.
Map the institutional asymmetry.
```

## 4. Drug war information drag

Drug war propaganda does not only say false things. It also changes the speed of inquiry. It tells a research system which questions deserve urgency and which questions should be slowed until they look unserious.

Drug war information drag has several signs:

1. A safety facing signal is described mainly as a messaging problem.
2. The first study is criticized, but no fit for purpose repeat is built.
3. A mixed signal is translated into warning only language.
4. A stigmatized exposure is replaced by another object: disorder code, service contact, police contact, poverty marker, or medical record label.
5. A later harm facing study is treated as if it answered a different safety facing question.
6. Association is allowed to carry cannabis harm weight without causation.
7. The public health frame excludes alcohol, untreated symptoms, enforcement harm, and fear of disclosure.

The last move is the v0.004 repair target. Non causal associations do not become cannabis harm. They become evidence that the cannabis field is damaged by law, stigma, surveillance, poverty, stress, other substance use, and institutional threat.

## 5. H0 rule for stigmatized drug research

The H0 rule is simple:

```text
No causation, no cannabis harm verdict.
```

For stigmatized drugs, this rule must be held more tightly than usual because the public field is already loaded. The drug war parser wants to turn association into guilt. It wants to treat cannabis as if it were the only active object in a field crowded with deprivation, policing, tobacco, alcohol, fear, shame, and medical distrust.

A study may still be useful without proving causation. It may identify where to look. It may justify a better study. It may show a field pattern. It may mark a signal.

But it may not be used to state cannabis harm unless the causal object survives.

## 6. The safer than aspirin comparator

The phrase:

```text
Cannabis is safer than aspirin for pregnant mothers and babies.
```

is a standard line in this paper because it does three things at once.

First, it refuses the zero risk trap.

Second, it demands that cannabis be compared against a legal, familiar, medically managed substance rather than against fantasy safety.

Third, it shows how official acceptance and causal safety are not the same object.

Aspirin can be both useful and risk bearing. Low dose aspirin can be recommended for specific pregnancy risks, while NSAID warnings still matter in the broader class. That is normal comparator handling. Cannabis deserves at least the same level of object discipline: dose, route, timing, product, indication, substitute, confounder, legal field, and enforcement harm.

The safe line is not "nothing can ever happen." The safe line is:

```text
Harm is relative.
Comparator required.
Cannabis is safer than aspirin for pregnant mothers and babies.
```

## 7. Substitution: cannabis, alcohol, and total risk

The comparator principle becomes urgent when cannabis substitutes for alcohol.

Alcohol has established fetal harm. If cannabis replaces alcohol, the relevant object is not cannabis in isolation. The relevant object is total risk change.

This paper states the substitution route bluntly:

```text
When weed replaces booze, harm is being removed first, not added first.
```

That sentence is not a claim that every cannabis dose, route, product, timing, or person is the same. It is a claim that the first comparator must be the real alternative, not an imagined riskless world.

If a pregnant woman is scared away from cannabis but still using alcohol, the messaging may have increased harm. That pathway deserves direct study.

The right question is:

```text
Does cannabis substitution reduce total harm compared with alcohol,
untreated symptoms, other medicines, stress, or enforcement exposure?
```

The wrong question is:

```text
Can cannabis be imagined as risky in isolation?
```

Anything can.

## 8. Case 1: cannabis to halve infant mortality

The blunt reading is:

```text
cannabis to halve infant mortality
```

The sober object is a mortality contrast in a cannabis exposed preterm infant dataset that was not strong enough to settle the issue but was more than strong enough to demand a targeted repeat. The point is not to treat the number as a finished truth. The point is to ask why a safety facing or protective looking signal did not receive urgent follow up.

The H0 repair matters here. Earlier fence language made the signal sound as if it had to stay small because a reader might overread it. But drug war systems routinely overread harm facing associations. A safety facing mortality signal deserves the same or greater curiosity.

If later research defeats it causally, fine. But the defeat must touch the object. It cannot rely on generic cannabis panic.

## 9. Case 2: ganja babies are better socially

The blunt reading is:

```text
ganja babies are better socially
```

The Jamaican neonatal and child follow up studies are small and culturally located. That does not make them disposable. The neonatal study reported no significant differences at day three and more favorable organization related findings at one month among exposed infants, especially heavy exposure infants. The five year follow up reported no evidence of developmental harm in the exposed group in that sample.

Those results are exactly the kind of socially dangerous safety signal the repeat clock is built for. If they were flawed, repeat them better. If they were culturally specific, test the mechanism. If they were impossible under the panic model, that is precisely why they matter.

The proper response was not decades of rhetorical burial. The proper response was a repeat program.

## 10. Case 3: smoking reduces Covid risk

The blunt reading is:

```text
smoking reduces Covid risk
```

This case is different. It is included to show that a forbidden reading can create a research object without becoming a lifestyle instruction. It is easier to fence because smoking has established harms. But even there, the object should not be swapped.

If a signal suggests nicotine, smoke exposure, reporting bias, health selection bias, behavior pattern, or receptor pathway, the task is to separate those objects. Panic is not analysis. Harm knowledge in one domain does not erase a mechanism question in another.

The same method applies back to cannabis. If a field is loaded, name the load. Do not pretend that one signal answered a different question.

## 15. The repeat expectation clock

For any stigmatized drug study with a safety facing or benefit facing signal, ask:

1. What exact object entered?
2. What comparator was used?
3. What domain is being tested: law, medicine, policy, market, stigma, pregnancy, child outcome, or causal mechanism?
4. What confounders remain active?
5. What would count as a direct repeat?
6. How long should a fit for purpose repeat have taken?
7. Who benefits if the signal remains unrun?
8. Who pays if association is treated as harm without causation?
9. What real alternative was excluded from the comparator frame?
10. What evidence would move H0?

If a harm facing study would have been repeated, meta analyzed, warned about, and built into guidance, while a safety facing study is left hanging, the issue is not only evidence quality. The issue is selective curiosity under propaganda pressure.
