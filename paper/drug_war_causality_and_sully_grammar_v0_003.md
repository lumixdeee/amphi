# Drug-War Causality and Sully Grammar

## Status-Contaminated Causality and Route Medicine

Version: v0.003
Date: 2026-07-04
Status: hypothesis / method paper, not medical advice, not legal advice, not harm denial.
Source: v0.002 grooved by review packet.

---

## Abstract

Drug-war causality is a method failure in that moral, legal, and institutional status is allowed to behave like biological evidence.

It says:

```text
bad_object touched_body;
bad_outcome appeared;
therefore bad_object caused_outcome.
```

This paper names that move sully grammar.

Export-safe alias:

```text
status_contaminated_causality
```

The target is not medicine. The target is captured medicine: bad-object thinking wearing clinical clothing. A drug can have real effects. A drug can increase danger. A drug can trigger, amplify, mask, or complicate harm. None of that licenses a jump from law label, stigma, service contact, or public fear to a single causal object.

The public-facing repair term is:

```text
route_medicine
```

Route medicine separates:

```text
exposure;
dose;
product;
route;
frequency;
timing;
context;
co_substances;
withdrawal;
reintroduction;
service_contact;
enforcement_contact;
diagnostic_capture;
reverse_path;
shared_liability;
claim_size;
rights_burden.
```

The question is not:

```text
is the drug good or bad?
```

The question is:

```text
what claim size has this route earned?
```

Keeper:

```text
The drug may matter.
The route decides how.
The law label does not get to answer for the body.
```

## 2. Core claim

Drug-war thinking turns object status into cause status.

```text
bad_object
+ contact
+ bad_outcome
= cause
```

That is sully grammar, or status-contaminated causality.

It is not science. It is a social grammar that can enter law, press language, clinical notes, registry variables, public health slogans, family stories, and service pathways.

Better route:

```text
exposure
+ dose_product_route_frequency
+ timing
+ comparator
+ counterroutes
+ contact_field_audit
+ rights_burden_if_control_claim
= bounded_claim
```

The goal is not to protect a drug from criticism.

The goal is to protect cause from being eaten by status.

## 3. Sully grammar / status-contaminated causality

Sully grammar compresses many routes into one bad object.

```text
SULLY_GRAMMAR={
  law_label -> danger_aura;
  danger_aura -> cause_assumption;
  cause_assumption -> diagnosis_shorthand;
  diagnosis_shorthand -> public_slogan;
  public_slogan -> future_prior;
}
```

Status-contaminated causality is the export-safe form.

```text
STATUS_CONTAMINATED_CAUSALITY={
  moral_status enters causal inference;
  legal_status acts like exposure data;
  stigma acts like mechanism;
  service_contact acts like incidence;
  public_object acts like biological object;
}
```

This can happen even when the starting association is real.

That is why the problem is not simple denial versus science.

The real task is claim custody.

```text
association may survive;
public object may fail;
bounded route may survive;
bad-object cause may fail.
```

## 4. Stigma does not erase risk

This rule must stay near the front.

```text
stigma_can_inflate_causal_certainty;
stigma_does_not_erase_biological_risk.
```

A stigmatized drug can still cause harm.

A legal drug can still cause harm.

An illegal drug can have a real pharmacological route.

A legal label can still distort public perception.

The audit does not ask whether the object deserves sympathy.

It asks what route has been measured.

```text
anti_stigma != anti_risk
anti_drug_war != anti_medicine
route_audit != harm_denial
```

## 6. Exposure and co-substance gate

A drug label is not an exposure.

Exposure audit asks:

```text
EXPOSURE_AUDIT={
  product;
  dose;
  route;
  frequency;
  active_ingredients;
  potency;
  tolerance;
  supply_route;
  age_window;
  sleep_state;
  stress_state;
  co_substances;
  medication_changes;
  withdrawal;
  reintroduction;
}
```

Drug-war fields distort disclosure.

Some exposures are easier to admit than others. Some carry heavier punishment, shame, family cost, service cost, or legal danger. Some are less likely to be recorded.

Displacement route:

```text
harder_to_report_exposure
+ easier_to_record_label
+ crisis
= easier_label_absorbs_causal_blame
```

Gate:

```text
CO_SUBSTANCE_GATE={
  what_else_was_used;
  what_else_was_stopped;
  what_else_was_hidden;
  what_else_was_unavailable_to_record;
  what_substance_was_socially_easiest_to_blame;
  what_substance_was_clinically_easiest_to_code;
  what_label_absorbed_the_mess;
}
```

No strong drug-harm claim should bypass this gate.

## 7. Contact-field gate

If a person's route to diagnosis runs through police, emergency services, mandated treatment, school discipline, housing threat, employment threat, or family surveillance, the outcome variable may partly record contact-field intensity.

```text
CONTACT_FIELD_GATE={
  who_is_seen;
  who_is_searched;
  who_is_believed;
  who_is_coded;
  who_is_punished;
  who_delays_help;
  who_discloses;
  who_loses_housing_work_family_position;
}
```

A dataset can be accurate about recorded contact and incomplete about underlying incidence.

That is not a reason to discard the dataset.

It is a reason to stop pretending the dataset sees without a route.

## 8. Reverse path and shared liability

Sully grammar prefers one arrow.

```text
drug -> bad_outcome
```

Route medicine keeps more arrows active.

```text
REVERSE_PATH={
  distress -> use;
  sleep_loss -> use;
  prodrome -> use;
  trauma -> use;
  social_defeat -> use;
  self_medication -> use;
  pain -> use;
  exclusion -> use;
}
```

```text
SHARED_LIABILITY={
  genetic_liability;
  neurodevelopmental_load;
  trauma_exposure;
  poverty;
  exclusion;
  stress;
  family_history;
  sleep_vulnerability;
  prior_psychiatric_route;
}
```

A drug may still matter after these gates.

But the claim gets smaller, sharper, and more useful.

## 9. Claim-size ladder

Different claims require different gates.

```text
ASSOCIATION_CLAIM:
  says: A and B are statistically linked.
  needs: exposure and outcome definitions, confounds, measurement limits.

BOUNDED_HARM_CLAIM:
  says: some route of A may raise probability of B.
  needs: dose, product, timing, comparator, plausible mechanism, alternatives.

TRIGGER_CLAIM:
  says: A may precipitate B in some people.
  needs: time course, vulnerability route, other triggers, cautious re-exposure logic.

COMPONENT_CAUSE_CLAIM:
  says: A contributes inside a causal constellation.
  needs: interaction map, subgroup boundary, no necessary/sufficient inflation.

UNITARY_PUBLIC_CAUSE_CLAIM:
  says: A causes B as a public object.
  needs: highest burden; often fails.

CONTROL_CLAIM:
  says: A justifies punishment, surveillance, forced disclosure, exclusion,
  or coercive treatment.
  needs: science burden plus rights burden; association alone cannot carry it.
```

Rule:

```text
claim_size must not exceed route_payment.
```

## 10. Rights burden for control claims

A science claim and a control claim are not the same thing.

Even where a drug route increases harm probability, punishment or coercive control does not automatically follow.

```text
RIGHTS_BURDEN={
  necessity;
  proportionality;
  least_restrictive_alternative;
  non_discrimination;
  evidence_of_benefit;
  evidence_of_lower_harm_alternatives;
  affected_person_voice;
  appeal_or_review_path;
  time_limit;
  exit_path;
}
```

A control claim must answer:

```text
What harm is being prevented?
What evidence says this control prevents it?
What lower-harm route was tried or rejected?
Who bears the burden?
Who is heard?
Who exits?
What damage does the control itself create?
```

Association alone cannot carry coercion.

Route medicine separates:

```text
health_warning;
clinical_support;
public_health_planning;
rights_restricting_control.
```

Each has a different burden.

## 11. Route medicine vs bad-object medicine

Bad-object medicine has a recognizable grammar.

```text
BAD_OBJECT_MEDICINE={
  names_exposure_after_law;
  treats_illegality_as_biological_severity;
  treats_fear_as_mechanism;
  treats_stigma_as_prior_probability;
  treats_confession_as_exposure_measurement;
  treats_arrest_or_contact_as_incidence;
  treats_route_questions_as_denial;
}
```

Route medicine does the opposite.

```text
ROUTE_MEDICINE={
  defines_exposure;
  defines_outcome;
  bounds_claim_size;
  checks_timing;
  checks_body_cost;
  checks_action_danger;
  checks_comparator;
  checks_alternatives;
  separates_help_from_blame;
  separates_warning_from_control;
}
```

Medicine becomes stronger when it stops borrowing certainty from stigma.

## 12. Worked example A: cannabis and psychosis

The cannabis-psychosis domain is useful because the association is not imaginary.

A bounded statement may survive:

```text
frequent or high-THC cannabis exposure can be associated with higher psychosis probability,
especially in vulnerable contexts.
```

But "cannabis causes psychosis" can mean many different objects.

```text
CAN_PSY_MEANINGS={
  acute_intoxication_panic_or_paranoia;
  transient_substance_induced_psychotic_symptoms;
  sleep_loss_or_stress_amplified_crisis;
  high_THC_frequent_use_route;
  adolescent_developmental_window_route;
  vulnerable_subgroup_trigger_route;
  reverse_path_or_self_medication_route;
  shared_liability_route;
  co_substance_route;
  enforcement_or_service_contact_route;
  diagnostic_capture_route;
  public_slogan;
  commercial_potency_route;
}
```

These are not the same object.

Good paper sentence:

```text
Some cannabis exposure routes may contribute to psychosis probability.
The route must specify product, dose, timing, frequency, vulnerability,
co-substances, reverse path, shared liability, and contact field.
```

Bad paper sentence:

```text
Cannabis is the cause object.
```

That second sentence is sully grammar.

## 16. Public-health slogan card

A public-health slogan can warn, simplify, and move fast.

It cannot become a causal court.

```text
SLOGAN_CARD={
  slogan:
  population:
  exposure:
  outcome:
  claim_size:
  route_paid:
  route_unpaid:
  uncertainty:
  what_it_cannot_decide:
  misuse_risk:
  rights_limit:
}
```

Example:

```text
slogan:
  high-THC frequent cannabis use can raise psychosis probability in some groups

population:
  people using frequent high-THC products, especially with vulnerability routes

exposure:
  high-THC / high-frequency use, not cannabis as a total object

outcome:
  psychosis probability or psychosis-like crisis, not all distress

claim_size:
  bounded harm claim

route_paid:
  association and plausible product-frequency concern

route_unpaid:
  sole cause, every case, user blame, control claim

uncertainty:
  co-substances, reverse path, shared liability, contact field

what_it_cannot_decide:
  individual cause, legal blame, coercive policy

misuse_risk:
  law label becomes medical explanation

rights_limit:
  warning does not equal punishment
```

Bound slogan:

```text
Higher-frequency, higher-THC cannabis use may increase psychosis probability in some groups.
This does not mean every cannabis-associated crisis has one cause,
and it does not let law labels substitute for exposure data.
```

## 17. Source-rent cards

Each source gets a rent card.

```text
SOURCE_RENT_CARD={
  source:
  source_layer:
  route_it_can_support:
  route_it_cannot_support:
  allowed_claim:
  blocked_claim:
  next_test:
}
```

Example:

```text
source:
  cannabis_psychosis_association_review

source_layer:
  epidemiology

route_it_can_support:
  statistical association;
  higher-frequency or higher-potency concern;
  vulnerable-subgroup concern.

route_it_cannot_support:
  law label as cause;
  every crisis as cannabis-caused;
  user blame;
  single public object;
  coercive control by itself.

allowed_claim:
  some cannabis exposure routes may increase psychosis probability.

blocked_claim:
  cannabis as one bad object caused the person.

next_test:
  product, THC/CBD, dose, frequency, age, sleep, stress,
  co-substances, reverse path, shared liability, service contact.
```

Source rent stops useful sources from becoming bad-object engines.

## 18. Sully audit

Before accepting a drug-harm claim, ask:

```text
SULLY_AUDIT={
  is_exposure_product_route_or_law_label;
  is_outcome_symptom_crisis_diagnosis_service_contact_or_life_course;
  is_claim_association_trigger_component_cause_sole_cause_or_control;
  are_dose_timing_route_product_potency_frequency_available;
  are_co_substances_and_disclosure_pressures_handled;
  are_policing_and_service_contact_routes_separated_from_incidence;
  are_reverse_path_and_shared_liability_handled;
  is_commercial_or_normalization_field_distorting_warning;
  is_stigma_doing_hidden_causal_work;
  what_would_weaken_claim;
  what_would_strengthen_claim;
}
```

If most answers are unknown, the claim may still work as a warning.

It has not earned the right to become a total object.

## 21. Falsifiers

The paper should take damage if:

```text
F1:
  drug-war status does not affect exposure recording, service contact,
  public causal certainty, or clinical shorthand.

F2:
  law labels never behave as causal priors in drug-harm discourse.

F3:
  co-substance and disclosure gates do not change interpretation
  of strong public claims.

F4:
  contact-field audit never changes apparent incidence or outcome interpretation.

F5:
  route-audited claims are not more accurate or more useful than bad-object claims.

F6:
  legalization, commercialization, or normalization fields never create
  their own warning or measurement distortions.

F7:
  rights-burden analysis does not change control-claim acceptability.

F8:
  the sully grammar handle becomes its own bad object
  and starts dismissing real drug harms.
```

F8 matters.

This paper is not allowed to become denial grammar.

It is bad method.

It lets status impersonate cause. It lets fear impersonate mechanism. It lets stigma impersonate evidence. It lets a public object eat the route.

The repair is not silence.

The repair is not denial.

The repair is route custody.

```text

stigma != evidence
association != cause
warning != verdict
health_warning != control_claim
```
